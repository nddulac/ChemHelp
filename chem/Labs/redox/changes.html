Electron exchange reactions

Many reactions involve the exchange of electrons. We know, for example, that when a neutral metal atom loses an electron, the atom forms a positve ion. The charge on the atom changes from zero to +1. At the same time, an electron is freed to interact with another atom or molecule.

Another example of a chemical change which involves an exchange of electrons is the formation of an anion from a neutral nonmetal atom. This occurs by the atom collecting an electron. The resulting ion will have a negative charge. The electron which is collected by the nonmetal atom must come from someplace.

For this reason, a change which involves an atom or molecule giving up an electron (or more than one elctron) is always accompanied by a change which involves an atom or molecule gaining an electron (or more than one elctron). These processes always occur in pairs.

This module will discuss how we as chemists describe chemical changes which involve an exchange of electrons.
