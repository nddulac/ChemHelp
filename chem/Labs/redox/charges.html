Comments on the Charges of Ions

Keep in mind that the charge on an ion has to do with the numbers of electrons and protons.

Consider a sodium atom. A sodium atom has 11 protons, 12 neutrons and 11 electrons. Because the number of electrons and protons are equal, the electrical charge due to the protons (+11) is exactly cancelled by that due to the electrons (-11). If the atom loses an electron, the electrical charge due to the protons (+11) will no longer be cancelled by that of the electrons (-10) and the resulting ion will have a charge of +1.
