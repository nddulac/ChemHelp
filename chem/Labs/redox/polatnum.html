Oxidation Numbers in Polyatomic Ions and Compounds

The sum of the oxidation numbers of all of the atoms in a compound or ion must be the same as the charge. Thus, in the carbonate ion (CO3-2) the sum of the oxidation number on carbon plus three times the oxidation number on oxygen must equal -2.

(#C) + 3(#O) = -2

Since the oxidation number of oxygen is always -2, we can make that substitution.

(#C) + 3(-2) = -2

Adding 6 to both sides gives

(#C) = +4

This method can be used to find the oxidation number of the central atom in any oxyanion.

This method can also be used to find the oxidation number of the central atom in ions containing other elements whose oxidation numbers we know.

What is the oxidation number on copper in CuF4-2? The sum of the oxidation number on copper plus four times the oxidation number on fluorine must equal -2.

(#Cu) + 4(#F) = -2

The oxidation number on fluorine is always -1, so we can make that substitution.

(#Cu) + 4(-1) = -2

Adding 4 to both sides gives

(#Cu) = +2

And we see that the oxidation number on copper in CuF4-2 is+2.

2(#S) + 3(#O) = -2

 There is nothing special about ions. Remember that the charge on a neutral molecule is 0. Thus the sum of all of the oxidation numbers of the atoms in a neutral molecule must equal 0.

What is the oxidation number on nitrogen in NF3? The sum of the oxidation number on nitrogen plus three times the oxidation number on fluorine must equal 0.

(#N) + 3(#F) = 0

The oxidation number on fluorine is always -1, so we can make that substitution.

(#N) + 3(-1) = 0

Adding 3 to both sides gives

(#N) = +3

What is the oxidation number on carbon in the difluoromethane (CH2F2)? The sum of the oxidation number on carbon plus two times the oxidation number on hydrogen plus twice the oxidation number onfluorine must equal 0.

(#C) + 2(#H) + 2(#F) = 0

Since the oxidation number of fluorine is always -1, we can make that substitution.

(#C) + 2(#H) +2(-1) = 0

Adding 2 to both sides gives

(#C) + 2(#H) = +2

Since the oxidation number on hydrogen is always +1, we can make that substitution.

(#C) + 2(+1) = +2

Subtracting 2 from both sides gives

(#C) = 0

And thus we see that the oxidation number on carbon in difluoromethane is 0.

An element can have many different oxidation numbers. The oxidation number of an atom depends on the surronding atoms. Consider a series of anions all of which have chlorine as the central atom.

Ion Ox. # on Cl
ClO4- +7
ClO3- +5
ClO2- +3
ClO- +1
Cl- -1

It is interesting to note that the oxidation number on chlorine increases with an increasing number of oxygens. In other words, the chlorine is more oxidized as more oxygen atoms are added. It is easy to see why increasing the oxidation number is called "oxidation"!
