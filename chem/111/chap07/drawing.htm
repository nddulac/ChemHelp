On Drawing Lewis Structures

Rules for Drawing

Lewis structures are easy to draw if you follow a simple procedure:

  1. Count the electrons.
    • Each atom will contribute its valence electrons.
    • You may need to adjust the number of electrons if the system is an ion
      • Add electrons ot account for a negative charge.
      • Subtract electrons to account for a positive charge.
  2. Draw a skeletal structure, connecting atoms with single bonds.
    • Each single bonds accoutns for two electrons.
  3. Place remaining electrons as non-bonding pairs, filling the octets of outside atoms first.
  4. Form multiple bonds or expanded octets as needed.
    • If too few electrons remain to fill the octets of inner atoms, move non-bonding pairs inward to form multiple bonds.
    • If there are extra electrons, place them as non-bonding pairs on atoms that can support expanded octets (period 3 and below.)
