Half Reactions

After indentifying the species which are oxidizing and reducing, it is possible to separate the overall reaction into half reactions. One half reaction will contain the part of the reaction which involves oxidation and the other will contain that which involves reduction.

Consider the reaction

Cu + 2 AgNO3 --> Cu(NO3)2 + 2 Ag

First, for simplicity, lets neglect the spectator ion (nitrate) and write the net ionic equation

Cu + 2 Ag+ --> Cu+2 + 2 Ag

Copper is oxidizing. The half reaction for the oxidation of copper in this process is

Cu --> Cu+2 + 2 e-

Similarly, silver is reducing. The half reaction for the reduction of silver is

Ag+ + e- --> Ag

Note that a different number of electrons is involved in each half reaction. Two atoms of silver must be reduced for every atom of copper which is oxidized since all of the electrons must cancel out in the overall process.

Some half reactions are more complex and involve more species.
