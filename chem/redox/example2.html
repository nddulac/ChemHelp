Example: Oxidizing and Reducing Agents

Consider the reaction:
2 FeCl3 + SnCl2 --> 2 FeCl2 + SnCl4

What is oxidizing?
The oxidation number on tin is changing from +2 to +4. Since its oxidation number is getting larger, it is clear that tin is oxidizing.

What is reducing?
The oxidation number on iron is changing from +3 to +2. Iron is reducing.

What is chloride doing?
The oxidation number on chlorine is -1 on both the reactant side of the equation and on the product side. Since its oxidation number is not changing, chlorine is neither oxidizing nor reducing. Chlorine, in this example, is a spectator ion.

What is the oxidizing agent?
The oxidizing agent is taking the electrons from the species which is oxidizing. Since iron is reducing, the reactant which contains iron must be the oxidizing agent. Iron (III) chloride is the oxidizing agent.

What is the reducing agent?
The reducing agent is the source of electrons for the species which is reduced in the reaction. Thus, the reducing agent must contain the the species which is oxidizing. Tin (II) chloride is the reducing agent.

What is oxidizing? tin
What is reducing? iron
Oxidizing agent iron (III) chloride
Reducing agent tin (II) chloride
Chloride is a spectator ion.
