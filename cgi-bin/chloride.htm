An Unknown Chloride

In this experiment, you will determine the percent mass of an unknown sample that is due to chloride. The sample is a mixture of NaCl and MgCl2.

The Procedure is as follows:

  1. Obtain about 1 g of an uknown sample.
  2. Dissolve the sample in water.
  3. Precipitate all of the chloride by reacting with an excess fo AgNO3.
  4. Filter and dry the nacient AgCl(s).

Based on the results, you should be able to calculate

  • the number of moles of Cl- in the original sample
  • the percent by mass of the original sample due to chlorine
  • the percent by mass of the original sample due to sodium chloride

Useful Information

Chlorine 35.45 g/mol
NaCl 58.44 g/mol
MgCl2 95.21 g/mol
AgCl 143.32 g/mol
